About Me

I am a Undergrad Double Majoring in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics at CU Boulder. I am doing my thesis work in the Autonomous Robotics and Perception Group in the Department of Computer Science, focusing on the intersection of semantic understanding and Simultanious Localization and Mapping (SLAM). I also work in the Autonomous Vehicle Systems Laboratory in the Department of Aerospace Engineering primarily on the visualization of Basilisk, a high fidelity, high performance astrodynamics simulation framework in Python.

I am currently seeking a lab in which to pursue a PhD. My primary research intrests thus far are in Machine Learning, Robotic Perception, Computer Vision and Collaborative Perception.

In my free time, I like reading, playing video games (specifically League of Legends right now) and spending time with my friends.
